## Publishing resources
- William P. Germano, *Getting It Published: A Guide for Scholars and Anyone Else Serious about Serious Books*, Third Edition (The University of Chicago Press, 2016). The Fourth edition is coming out February 2026.
- William P. Germano, *From Dissertation to Book*, Second Edition (The University of Chicago Press, 2013).
- Laura Portwood-Stacer, *The Book Proposal Book: A Guide for Scholarly Authors* (Princeton University Press, 2021).
- Laura Portwood-Stacer, [Manuscript Works](https://manuscriptworks.com): Includes a mailing list with free content and courses on publishing and writing.

## Open Access publishing and Public Humanities
- Peter Suber, *Open Access* (The MIT Press, 2012), <https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/9286.003.0017>.
- Kathleen Fitzpatrick, *Generous Thinking: A Radical Approach to Saving the University* (Johns Hopkins University Press, 2021).
- Martin Paul Eve and Jonathan Gray, eds., *Reassembling Scholarly Communications: Histories, Infrastructures, and Global Politics of Open Access* (The MIT Press, 2020), <https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/11885.001.0001>.
- *Dædalus*, v 151 no. 3 (2022): [The Humanities in American Life: Transforming the Relationship with the Public](https://www.jstor.org/stable/e48510589).
- *The Journal of Electronic Publishing*, v. 26 no. 1 (2023): [Open Access](https://journals.publishing.umich.edu/jep/issue/195/info/).
- *Public Humanities*, v. 1 (2025): [Manifesto Issue](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/public-humanities/issues/the-manifesto-issue).
	- Particularly relevant is Daniel Fisher-Livne et al., "The Revolution Must Be Published: Toward a Radically Inclusive Publishing Ecology," *Public Humanities* 1 (2025): 1–6, <https://doi.org/10.1017/pub.2024.18>.

## Open Access Infrastructure
- [Principles of Open Scholarly Infrastructure](https://openscholarlyinfrastructure.org/)
- [Knowledge Commons](https://hcommons.org): Set of tools to support academics in connecting, creating, and sharing knowledge. It is a project funded by Michigan State University, MLA, and (formally) by NEH grants.
	- [Knowledge Commons Works](https://works.kcommons.org): Open Access repository
- [OAPEN - Open Access Publishing in European Networks](https://www.oapen.org)
- [COPIM – Community-led Open Publication Infrastructures for Monographs](https://www.copim.ac.uk)

## Open Access aggregators
- [JSTOR Open Access books and journals](https://about.jstor.org/oa-and-free/)
- [Project Muse - Open Access books and journals](https://about.muse.jhu.edu/muse/open-access-overview/)
- [DOAJ - Directory of Open Access Journals](https://doaj.org)
- [De Gruyter UPL Open](https://uplopen.com)
- [DOAB - Directory of Open Access Books](https://doabooks.org): A directory of peer reviewed open access books and book publishers. It provides an indexing service with the aim of being a global and reliable source for OA book metadata and OA publishers.
- [OAPEN Library - Online library of open access books](https://www.oapen.org): Hosts OA books.
- [Open Book Collective - COPIM](https://openbookcollective.org): Hosts OA books.
