## Resources on critical data literacy
- Bergstrom, Carl T., and Jevin D. West. "Modern-Day Oracles or Bullshit Machines?" 2025. <https://thebullshitmachines.com/>.
- Bergstrom, Carl T., and Jevin D. West. *Calling Bullshit: The Art of Skepticism in a Data-Driven World*. Random House, 2021. <https://www.callingbullshit.org>.
- Bigo, Didier, Engin F. Isin, and Evelyn Sharon Ruppert, eds. *Data Politics: Worlds, Subjects, Rights*. Routledge, 2019. <https://library.oapen.org/handle/20.500.12657/24706>.
- Broussard, Meredith. *Artificial Unintelligence: How Computers Misunderstand the World*. The MIT Press, 2018. <https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/11022.001.0001>.
- D'Ignazio, Catherine, and Lauren F. Klein. *Data Feminism*. The MIT Press, 2020. <https://data-feminism.mitpress.mit.edu>.
- Foucault, Michel. *The Order of Things: An Archaeology of the Human Sciences*. Vintage, 1994.
- Fourcade, Marion, and Kieran Healy. *The Ordinal Society*. Harvard University Press, 2024. <https://doi.org/10.4159/9780674296688>.
- Gitelman, Lisa, ed. *"Raw Data" Is an Oxymoron*. MIT Press, 2013. <https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/9302.001.0001>.
- Newfield, Christopher, Anna Alexandrova, and Stephen John, eds. *Limits of the Numerical: The Abuses and Uses of Quantification*. The University of Chicago Press, 2022.
- O’Neil, Cathy. *Weapons of Math Destruction: How Big Data Increases Inequality and Threatens Democracy*. Crown, 2017.
- Porter, Theodore. *Trust in Numbers: The Pursuit of Objectivity in Science and Public Life*. Princeton University Press, 1995.
- Porter, Theodore M. *The Rise of Statistical Thinking, 1820–1900*. New Edition. Princeton University Press, 2020. <https://doi.org/10.2307/j.ctvxcrz1v>.

## Data and indigenous studies
- Andersen, Chris, Maggie Walter, Tahu Kukutai, and Chelsea Gabel. *Indigenous Statistics: From Data Deficits to Data Sovereignty*. 2nd edition. Routledge, 2025. <https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003173342>.
- [IndigeLab Network](https://www.indigelabnetwork.com): Research collectives that make room for Indigenous ways of knowing, doing, and being in the academy.
- [Collaborative Impact](https://collabimpact.org): Resources on participatory statistics and community driven data projects.
